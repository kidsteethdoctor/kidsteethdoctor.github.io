What is Pediatric Dentistry?

A pediatric dentist is the pediatrician of the dental world. They are dentists who have undergone extensive specialized training in child psychology, growth and development as well as the latest techniques for treating dental problems that occur during childhood. With each developmental stage, there are numerous changes within a child’s mouth. A pediatric dentist specializes in these changes, and is able to spot and treat problems before they ruin a child’s smile!

Our Nurturing Environment

Dr. Ching has created a nurturing environment where children feel safe, and parents find the warmth of a long-standing relationship with their child’s doctor. You will feel the benefits of their efforts from the minute you enter our child-centered waiting room. Welcomed by a friendly staff member into our brightly decorated reception area, your child will delight in the variety of toys, picture books and stuffed animals. Dr. Ching and his staff have been specially trained to gently calm the apprehensive child and answer all the questions of concerned parents.

Inside the operatory, all the latest and most advanced equipment is cleverly tucked away within a kid-friendly atmosphere. From the smallest infant to kids preparing for the SATs, there is something here to engage them all. Every treatment is carefully explained in a manner to match a child’s developmental stage so even the smallest toddler feels comfortable.

Prevention is Our Goal

Our emphasis is on prevention and early intervention. We believe a healthy smile is maintained through a long-term prevention plan. We monitor your child's growth and development to create a treatment program specific to your child's needs. As they grow, we will help them learn to care for their teeth. An early start, solid routine, and regular check-ups will ensure a lifetime of bright smiles.

New Technologies

We are continually updating to keep abreast of the latest in technology. We are proud to offer:

  • Digital radiography, which significantly decreases the amount of radiation exposure
  • Mercury-free composite resins
  • Porcelain crowns for baby teeth
  • Nitrous oxide
  • Oral conscious sedation
  • General anesthesia with a pediatric anesthesiologist from Children’s Hospital Oakland
  • Silver diamine fluoride
